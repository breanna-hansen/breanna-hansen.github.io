Cycle the Continental Divide Annual Event

August 8‐14, 2018

About the Event

Mountain Spoke challenges a fixed course annually through the Great Divide Mountain Bike Route (GDMBR) in it's annual event, Cycle the Continental Divide (CCD). Not only is the CCD a true classic, its 'primary track' is pursued with consistency by Mountain Spoke athletes so that year to year, finish times may always be compared directly to the record books.

About the Route

Following the Continental Divide as closely as practicable and crossing it 7 times, about 90% of the CCD is on unpaved roads and trails and requires basic off-pavement riding skills to complete. The unpaved portions of the route range from high quality dirt or gravel roads. While most of the CCD is off the pavement, the route does not require highly technical mountain bike riding skills. The route has been designed to provide a riding experience primarily on very low trafficked roads through mostly undeveloped areas of the Montana.

Details

  • Starting & Ending Town: Elkington, MT
  • Shuttle: A shuttle bus and trailer will transport riders to and from the starting point in Elkington, MT.
  • Meeting Place: Mountain Spoke
  • Tour Length: 150 to 325 miles
  • Daily Mileage Range: 15 to 92
  • Ability Level: Intermediate to Advanced
  • Group Size: 6 to 13 cyclists
  • Tour Cost: $2,795 per person. Group discount of $200 per person for groups of 4 or more.
  • Single Room Supplement: Add $470 to the Tour Cost (total for 5-nights) for a private room while on tour.

Tour Includes:

  • 6 days of guide service
  • 6 days of meals (5 dinners)
  • 5 nights of lodging
  • All taxes and entrance fees
  • Lunch en route daily
  • Energy food
  • Liquid refreshments
  • Shuttles and mechanical support

Rentals

Moutain Spoke is proud to rent Scott CR‐1 Pro and Scott Solace carbon road bikes with full Shimano Ultegra components for only $250/week. In addition we also have electric bikes available for rental at $375/week.

Fitness Ability

If one month prior to the tour, you can ride 2 to 3 times a week equaling a total of 75 or more miles a week, you'll do just fine.
