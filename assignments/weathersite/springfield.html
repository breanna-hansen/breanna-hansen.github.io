Springfield Zoo Adds New Exhibits

Springfield Zoo has added a new exhibit this year. Preparation have been under way for eight months to add a new giraffe enclosure. With construction now complete, zoo staff has welcome Gregory the giraffe for his Springfield debut. The official ribbon cutting ceremony will be held Tuesday, July 10th, at 4:00 p.m.

Zane Just, the Zoo manager, stated that Gregory's enclosure will be interactive and enjoyable both for animals and visitors. The housing barn features a gently slopping ramp up to the top level where visitors can be eye-level with Gregory. State-of-the-art cooling and misting fans will keep Gregory and future giraffe's cool in high temperatures. Safety railings around the inner walkways keep visitors safe but also allow them to be right near the giraffe. Included inside will also be several interactive features explaining about a giraffe's habitat, diet, and social habits.

While the zoo is very excited to welcome Gregory, they are eagerly anticipating him having a companion at some point. Funds are still being raised to have a second giraffe join the zoo. If you are interested in donating to the giraffe fund, please contact the Springfied Zoo at 384-358-3488, donate online at springfieldzoo.com, or donations can be accepted at the Springfield Zoo ticket office.
